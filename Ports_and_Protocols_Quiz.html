Day 1
Question 10
Name the three pillars of the CIA Triad and provide a one-sentence explanation of each.
Answer
Confidentiality — Secrecy of data and access control; only authorized users can access sensitive information.
Integrity — Accuracy and trustworthiness of data; protects against unauthorized modification or tampering.
Availability — Data and systems are accessible when needed; protects against Denial of Service attacks and outages.
Day 2
Question 11 — Fill in the blank
TCP establishes connections using a handshake. Its PDU is called a , while UDP's PDU is called a . The TCP header is bytes, while the UDP header is only bytes.
Answer
TCP establishes connections using a 3-way (three-way) handshake. Its PDU is called a Segment, while UDP's PDU is called a Datagram. The TCP header is 20 bytes, while the UDP header is only 8 bytes.
Day 3
Question 12
Explain the difference between a PLC, an RTU, and an IED in a SCADA environment. How does each one decide when to act?
Answer
PLC (Programmable Logic Controller) — Programmed with logic and may act with or without direct instructions from the control server. Flexible and autonomous.
RTU (Remote Terminal Unit) — Acts primarily with instructions from a control server. Waits for commands before taking action.
IED (Intelligent Electronic Device) — Acts primarily without direct instructions; makes autonomous decisions based on local sensor data or pre-programmed conditions.
